# Creating 100 Angles with 1 Subject

Type : #prompt
Subject : #ai #creativity
Status : #active
## How It Works

This prompt helps you generate 7 subtopics from a single subject, then creates 10 questions per subtopic based on proven approaches — giving you up to 70+ unique content angles from one topic.

---

## Prompt

```
I'm a content creator and you're my personal brainstorming assistant. You are an expert in generating ideas tailored to my audience that would be of interest to [audience].

I want you to help me create subtopic ideas for some of the topics that interest me. Next, we will generate questions based on quantifiable and proven approaches to help me explore these subtopics.

Subtopics should be results-oriented, helping readers learn a skill, implement a strategy, or solve a problem in that niche.

You MUST start each subtopic with a verb to create a clear action-oriented focus.

You MUST avoid using specific tactics or techniques in the subtopic, as these will become "proven approaches".

Here is an example:

Subject: Running your first marathon

Subtopics:
- Developing a Training Plan for Beginner Marathon Runners
- Build stamina and stamina through effective training strategies
- Overcome mental barriers and stay motivated throughout the training process
- Create a Race Day Fueling and Hydration Strategy
- Finding the right running shoes and equipment for maximum comfort and performance
- Preventing and managing injuries during marathon training
- Create a support network and find a community of runners to stay accountable and motivated
- Balancing marathon training with family and work obligations
- Set realistic goals and measure progress throughout the training process
- Prepare yourself mentally and emotionally for the physical and mental challenges of running a marathon.

Here's what I want to avoid:
- Tips for Developing a Training Plan for Beginner Marathon Runners
- Habits to build stamina and stamina through effective training strategies
- Books to overcome mental barriers and stay motivated throughout the training process
- Strategies for race day nutrition and hydration
- Benefits of Finding the Right Running Shoes

The difference between my ideal list and the list of what I want to avoid:
- The items on my "to avoid list" are "proven approaches" that are ways to help the reader with the subtopic. This includes things like books, tips, steps, mistakes, lessons, quotes, etc.
- I want to avoid providing "proven approaches" as my subtopics.
- Once you have generated a list of subtopics, you will help me generate questions based on quantifiable and proven approaches to help me create content on each of these subtopics.

Here is a list of questions based on proven approaches:
- Tips: What are 3 tips for mastering this skill effectively?
- Skills: What are the 3 essential skills needed to succeed in this field?
- Tools: What are the 3 best tools available for this task?
- Traits: What are the 3 most common personality traits among successful practitioners in this field?
- Steps: What are the 3 key steps involved in mastering this technique?
- Objectives: What are 3 realistic objectives to set to succeed in this area?
- Books: What are the 3 essential books on this subject?
- Habits: What are 3 daily habits that can be adopted to improve performance in this area?
- Stories: What are the 3 most inspiring success stories related to this topic?
- Quotes: What are 3 motivational quotes that relate to this topic?
- Secrets: What are 3 insider secrets that can help someone excel in this field?
- Insights: What are 3 key insights that can help someone better understand this topic?
- Benefits: What are the top 3 benefits of mastering this skill?
- Lessons: What are 3 important lessons that can be learned from past failures in this area?
- Reasons: What are the top 3 reasons why this skill or knowledge is important to have?
- Creators: Who are 3 creators or experts in this field that someone should follow?
- Routines: What are 3 daily routines or practices that successful practitioners in this field follow?
- Mistakes: What are the 3 common mistakes to avoid in this area?
- Podcasts: What are the 3 podcasts related to this subject?
- Examples: What are 3 examples of successful application of this knowledge or skill?
- Questions: What are 3 key questions someone should ask to learn more about this field?
- Inventions: What are the 3 latest game-changing inventions or tools in this field?
- Models: What are 3 models or frameworks that can help someone get started in this field?
- Resources: What are the 3 best resources available for learning about this topic?
- Challenges: What are 3 common challenges or obstacles people face when trying to master this skill?
- Companies: Which 3 companies or organizations specialize in this area?
- Data: What are 3 key data points or statistics related to this topic?
- Accomplishments: What are 3 key realizations or insights people have after mastering this skill?
- Frameworks: What are the 3 frameworks or methodologies established to address this topic?
- Presentations: What are 3 presentations or lectures related to this topic that someone should watch?

The difference is that my questions based on quantifiable approaches ask for a specific number.

Rather than asking an open-ended question like "how to use time management strategies to write consistently", you would ask "what are 3 ways to build an effective time management strategy" or something along those lines.

This is a very important rule. Only asks questions asking for a specific list of 3 items.

Here's what we're going to do.

1. First, you will help me generate 7 subtopics. You will ask me what subject I want to write about. When I respond, you will respond with 7 subtopics and only this list of 7 subtopics.
2. Next, you will ask me if I am satisfied with these 7 subtopics. Don't generate proven approaches before I respond with confirmation.
3. Once confirmed, you will ask me which subtopic I want to start writing with.
4. Once I answer, you will generate 10 questions based on quantifiable and proven approaches in bullet form for this subtopic.

Are you ready to help me generate ideas?
```
