# Zettelkasten Method SOP

**Goal:** Develop a connected, second-brain system by transforming notes into actionable and linked ideas, enhancing creativity and knowledge retention. Works in Eden, Obsidian, or any markdown-based app.

### Workflow Summary
1. **Capture Fleeting Notes**: Quickly capture ideas, thoughts, and highlights during content consumption.
2. **Create Literature Notes**: Summarize highlights into coherent notes that capture key concepts.
3. **Develop Permanent Notes**: Transform Literature Notes into atomic, permanent notes that represent singular, clear ideas.
4. **Connect Notes**: Link Permanent Notes using tags and connections to build a network of interconnected insights.

### Standard Metadata Fields
- **MOC**: Main Topic or Category
- **Type**: Note type — `#book`, `#article`, `#video`, `#podcast`, `#course` for literature notes; no type tag needed for permanent notes (the title is the identifier)
- **Subject**: Key topic covered — e.g., `#productivity`, `#mindset`, `#creativity`
- **Status**: The current state — see note statuses in Step 5
- **Author**: Source or creator of the content
- **Source**: Link to the original content if applicable
- **Date**: Date the note was created

### Step 1: Capturing Fleeting Notes
- **Definition**: Fleeting Notes are quick captures of ideas, thoughts, or highlights from content you consume. They are not fully formed ideas but rather initial observations or reflections.
- **When to Capture**: Capture Fleeting Notes whenever you highlight content during reading (Kindle), listening to podcasts (Snipd), or browsing articles (Reader).
- **How to Capture**: Use Tana on your phone (home screen widget) for instant capture on the go. Inside your note-taking app, use Obsidian's quick add or Eden's capture feature for desktop captures. Tag ideas to make them retrievable:
  - `#tweets` or `#youtube-idea` for content ideas
  - `#thought` for insights and reflections
  - Example: While walking, you have an idea for a newsletter — open Tana, tap the widget, write the idea, tag it. Done in 10 seconds.
  - Example: During a workout, you get a sudden insight about life. Note it with the tag `#thought` so it feeds your Galaxy later.

### Step 2: Creating Literature Notes
- **Definition**: Literature Notes are summaries of the highlights and thoughts captured from specific sources. They transform fleeting information into actionable knowledge, bridging the gap to Permanent Notes.
- **How to Create Literature Notes**:
1. **Select Content**: Choose content you've highlighted that you want to explore further. Follow your curiosity, but keep Lindy's Law and the Content Hierarchy in mind.
2. **Create a Literature Note**: Duplicate the appropriate Literature Note Template (Article, Book, Video, Podcast, or Course).
3. **Add Metadata**: Update the title and add relevant metadata from the standard metadata fields.
4. **Organize Notes**: Move the Literature Note to your "Notes" folder. (It can be alongside the Permanent Notes)
5. **Add Highlights**: Open the source material in a side pane and copy the most important highlights into the "Highlights" section of the note.
6. **Summarize Highlights**: Rewrite your highlights as individual concepts or ideas in the Summary section. Add separate sections for each concept or idea.
  1. Open the Literature Note in a side pane so you have your Highlights on one side and Summary on the other for easier summarizing.
7. **Repeat**: Continue this process until all important highlights are summarized or you feel satisfied.

### Step 3: Developing Permanent Notes
**Definition**: Permanent Notes are concise, stand-alone notes that represent a single concept or idea. They are long-lasting and designed to be easily linked to other notes, forming a connected knowledge base.

**Principle of Atomicity**: Ensure each note focuses on a single idea to make it easy to connect and use later.

**How to Create Permanent Notes**:
1. **Open the Literature Note**: Start by opening your Literature Note and selecting the first concept you want to create a Permanent Note on inside the Summary section.
2. **Duplicate the Template**: Duplicate the Permanent Note Template, rename it, move it to your main "Notes" folder, and add the appropriate metadata from the standard metadata fields.
3. **Copy & Paste Summary**: Copy and paste the content of your summary from the Literature Note into the "Big Idea" section of the Permanent Note.
4. **Enrich the Note (Optional)**: Enrich the Permanent Note using the additional sections:
- **Problem**
- **Goal**
- **Beliefs & Objections**
- **Benefits**
- **Example**
- **Process**
- **Concept**
- **Reference**
1. **Add Links**: While enriching the note, add links using double brackets `[[ ]]` or "@" to connect it with other notes if connections come to mind naturally.
2. **Finalize the Note**: When satisfied with the note, change the status tag from #enrich to #complete and proceed to Step 4: Connecting Notes.

### Step 4: Connecting Notes
- **Definition**: Connecting notes is the process of building a network of knowledge by creating links between related ideas.
- **How to Connect Notes**:
1. **Use Tags**: Add tags that reflect the key themes or categories of each note. Tags help in organizing and finding related ideas easily.
2. **Create Direct Links**: Link notes together where relevant. Use `[[ ]]` or "@" backlinks to draw connections between related Permanent Notes inside the "Links" section at the bottom of the notes.
3. **Trigger Questions**: Use trigger questions to find new connections:
- How does this note fit into what I already know?
- How can this note explain another concept already in my system?
- What idea contradicts this idea?
- Why am I attracted to this idea (apart from confirmation bias)?
- If I change the author's perspective, does this note evolve?
- What are the applications of this concept?
1. **Example**: Link a note about "Spaced Repetition" to a note on "The Forgetting Curve" to create a pathway between methods of effective learning.

### Step 5: Note Statuses and Prioritization
- **Note Statuses** (literature notes only):
  - `#inprogress` — Being actively read/highlighted
  - `#tosynthesize` — Ready to be transformed into permanent notes
  - `#complete` — Fully processed, no further action needed
- Permanent notes don't need a status — once it's written and connected, it's a permanent note. That's it.
- **Prioritizing Notes During Learning Sessions**:
  - When doing your daily or weekly learning routine, prioritize as follows:
1. **Notes to Synthesize** (`#tosynthesize`): Transform literature notes into permanent notes.
2. **Permanent Notes to Connect**: Add backlinks to strengthen your network of ideas.
3. **Consume New Content**: Using Reader, Snipd, etc.

### Step 6: Habit Building for the Zettelkasten Process
- **Daily Routine**:
1. **Capture Fleeting Notes** during content consumption (see the Content Acquisition SOP).
2. **Create Literature Notes** from Fleeting Notes at the end of each working day or during a dedicated time period.
3. **Develop Permanent Notes**:
- Once a week during a dedicated learning session to solidify key concepts.
- One note every day to gradually build your interconnected map of ideas.
1. **Connect Notes** weekly by revisiting and linking relevant ideas.
- **Tips for Habit Building**:
  - **Set a Routine**: Dedicate time each day for note transformation, such as 15 minutes after your working day.
  - **Gamify**: Track your progress by marking connections or using visualization tools (like Obsidian's graph view) to see how your network grows.
  - **Review Weekly**: Schedule a weekly review to revisit and connect older notes, strengthening the interconnected knowledge web.

### Action Steps for Immediate Implementation
1. **Set Up Your Workspace**: Make sure your note-taking app (Eden, Obsidian, etc.) and content capturing tools (Readwise, Snipd, Reader) are ready.
2. **Create Fleeting Notes**: Start capturing quick insights from daily content consumption.
3. **Set a Routine**: Schedule a daily or weekly time block for transforming Fleeting Notes into Literature and Permanent Notes.
4. **Link and Review**: Begin linking notes using backlinks to establish a connected knowledge system.

### Final Goal
By following this SOP, you will establish a robust second-brain system that helps transform fleeting information into organized, interconnected knowledge, enhancing creativity, retention, and application of what you learn.
