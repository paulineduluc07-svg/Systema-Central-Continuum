### Workflow Summary
1. **Select an Idea**
2. **Duplicate the Template**
3. **Brainstorm Topics**
4. **Brain Dump**
5. **Research**
6. **Ideation Process**
7. **Write Draft Outline**
8. **Hook Brainstorming**
9. **Write First Draft**
10. **Review Draft**
11. **Repurpose Content**

### Pre-Requisites
- Highlights inside your library (Readwise, Kindle, or your app's built-in library)
- Literature & Permanent Notes inside your workspace
- Content ideas captured in **Tana** with the appropriate tag (e.g., `#tweets`, `#youtube-idea`)

### Step 1: Select an Idea
- Open **Tana** and browse your captured content ideas. Filter by tag (e.g., `#tweets` for short posts, `#youtube-idea` for videos) to find ideas ready to develop.
- Pick the one with the most energy — the idea you're most excited to write about today.

### Step 2: Duplicate the Appropriate Template
1. **Duplicate the Template**: Choose either the Long-Form Template or a short-form template.
2. **Add Metadata**: Move it to your Content Creation folder, rename it according to your idea, and add metadata using the Tag Taxonomy:
- **Categories**: e.g., #newsletter, #youtube
- **Subjects**: e.g., #habits #sales
- **Date**: YYYY-MM-DD
- **Status**: #in-progress

### Step 3: Brainstorm Topics
- Define the topic(s) you want to write about based on your original idea (e.g., Sales, Productivity, Philosophy).

### Step 4: Brain Dump

**Option A — Manual (default)**
- Write down every idea and thought that comes to mind related to the original idea and the topic(s). Focus on getting everything out of your mind without worrying about order, structure, or clarity.
- **Tip**: Set a timer for 20 minutes and write continuously without stopping. The goal is to capture as many ideas as possible.

**Option B — AI Voice-Note Path (faster)**
1. Record a voice note on your phone — 3 to 10 minutes. Speak freely, as if explaining the idea to a smart friend over coffee. No filter, no structure.
2. Get the transcript (most voice memo apps auto-transcribe, or paste into Claude/ChatGPT).
3. Feed the transcript to AI with a prompt like: *"Structure this as a [newsletter / short post / YouTube outline]. Here are writing samples of mine to match my voice and tone: [paste 2-3 examples]."*
4. Review the AI output. It gives you the skeleton — you add stories, specific examples, your personality.
5. Continue from Step 5 (Research) or jump directly to Step 7 (Draft Outline) if the AI output is already structured enough.
- **Note**: Always provide your own writing samples to preserve voice. The AI handles structure; you handle the soul.

### Step 5: Research
1. **Search Within Your Second Brain**
- Copy-paste library highlights.
- Connect Literature Notes and Permanent Notes to identify existing knowledge.
- **Example**: Connect a Literature Note on productivity strategies and a Permanent Note about time management if you're writing about Productivity.
1. **Search Outside Your Second Brain**
  1. Research high-quality source material with Perplexity.ai
    1. Find studies, stories, articles or quotes that illustrate your points to add authority and social proof.
  2. Share your original outline and ideas with an AI assistant (Claude, ChatGPT) to brainstorm.
  3. Search for content related to the topic and save it to your reading app:
- Watch/read and highlight what's interesting. Copy-paste the highlights into your research section.
- Warning: Be wary not to switch from active researching to passive consumption!

### Step 6: The Ideation Process
- Brainstorm different writing angles to structure the ideas and concepts found during research. Fill in each section with 1-5 bullet points to illustrate them (not all need to be filled).
  - **Problem**: Illustrate and amplify a problem related to the topic.
  - **Goal**: List desirable goals people want to achieve and why they are important.
  - **Example**: Provide examples or personal experiences to make your writing relatable.
  - **Process**: List actionable steps for your key points to add value.
  - **Big Ideas**: Condense key points into big, attention-grabbing ideas (like a quote or summary).
  - **Concept**: Create names for processes or key points to make them more novel.
- **Tip**: Focus on elements that align most with your main message or audience needs.

### Step 7: Write the Draft Outline
1. Open the **Copywriting Structure Template** and select a structure that makes sense for the content.
- **Examples of Structures**:
  - **PAS**: Problem, Agitate, Solution
  - **APAG**: Attention, Perspective, Advantage, Gamify
1. Copy the key points section of the chosen structure into your document.
2. Open your ideation notes in a side pane. On the right, have your ideation elements; on the main pane, have the key points section.
3. Copy-paste or rewrite different bullet points into the key points following the chosen structure.
4. Re-read everything to ensure bullet points are in the right place and make sense. Aim for clarity, but don't stress — things will evolve as you draft.

### Step 8: Hook Brainstorming
- Brainstorm different types of hooks. Search for copywriting structures online, take inspiration from other creators, and ask an AI assistant for ideas based on the outline and subject.

### Step 9: Write the First Draft
1. Create a new document called "1st Draft" within the outline document (or alongside it).
2. Open the "1st Draft" in the main pane and the outline document in a side pane.
3. Start writing — this should be easy now that you've gone through the previous steps.
- **Tip**: Use your ideation and outline documents as a reference to maintain consistency and coherence.

### Step 10: Review Second Draft
1. Re-read your first draft out loud and:
  1. Rewrite sentences that don't flow smoothly or parts where you stumbled while reading out loud
  2. Aim to cut 10-20% for conciseness and impact.
2. Use tools like Grammarly or Hemingway to review mistakes and simplify sentences.
3. Share the first draft with an AI assistant for advice or suggestions on specific parts.
4. Continue revising until satisfied with the final version.

### Step 11: Repurpose Content
1. Open the final version.
2. Capture several standalone ideas from it (save them as notes tagged with #post-idea or similar).
3. Duplicate a short-form template for each idea.
4. Rename them, add them to the appropriate folder, and add the metadata.
5. Start writing each post:
- Use the **Copywriting Structure Template** if needed.
- Copy-paste specific parts of the newsletter and add hooks to make them stand-alone posts.
- Feed an AI assistant with copywriting structures, examples, and previously written content to create multiple posts from the original content — easily creating 10-30+ pieces of short-form content in 30 minutes to 1 hour.

### Habit-Building Tips
- **Daily Writing Practice**: Dedicate at least 30 minutes each day at the beginning of the day to writing or researching content.
- **Track Your Progress**: Use a habit-tracking app to monitor consistency and reward yourself for hitting milestones.
- **Use Separate Work Blocks**: Separate researching, outlining, and structuring ideas from writing the first draft. Ideally, outline the content and write the first draft the next day.

**Example Workflow**
1. **Outline & Research**: Monday
2. **First Draft Writing**: Tuesday
3. **Second Draft**: Wednesday
4. **Repurposing Final Content into Tweets/Reels**: Thursday
5. **Planning the Content**: Friday

**Note**: Repurposing content allows you to maximize reach and impact with minimal effort, transforming long-form content into bite-sized pieces suitable for social media.
